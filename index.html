Philosophy

The body communicates: my job is to listen

Every appointment is different, because your body needs something different every time you're on the table. Pressure, pace, and technique shift based on what's actually happening. Sometimes that calls for Graston or cupping on a stubborn issue, and sometimes it calls for slowing everything down.

This work supports people managing headaches, back pain, the toll of a desk job or a job on your feet all day, pregnancy, and the general wear of caring for a household. We hold a lot of tension, old and new, in our muscles, and part of this practice is learning to listen to what yours is telling us.

I'll always be honest with you: massage isn't a cure-all, and I can't promise to fix everything in a single visit. Think of it as a tool. I can help your body get the healing started, releasing what's holding on and getting things moving again, but the lasting work happens between sessions, in how you move, rest, and recover. Some issues are years in the making, so real, lasting change can take several visits. And if the habits that caused the tension stick around, it tends to find its way back.

And if you're simply here because a massage feels good and you want an hour to be taken care of, with no agenda and no homework, that's more than okay too. Either way, my goal is the same: that you leave feeling better than you came in.
