Services

Therapeutic & Swedish massage

Hot stone therapy

Lymphatic wellness massage

A light, rhythmic touch that supports your body's natural drainage system — easing swelling, encouraging detox, and leaving you feeling lighter.

Graston technique

Cupping

About your therapist

Meet Mary Irvine

Hi there — I'm Mary! I've been a massage therapist for over 11 years, with most of my experience rooted in the spa world, where I developed and refined my intuitive touch and relaxation techniques. Along the way I've worked alongside a physical therapist, gaining real manual-therapy skills, and collaborated with personal trainers and athletes — which deepened my understanding of movement, function, and body mechanics.

That mix lets me bring both a calming, restorative approach and a practical, therapeutic focus to every session. My passion comes from a deep fascination with the body and its natural ability to heal, and my goal is to help you learn to listen to your own body, so you understand what it needs to feel balanced and cared for. I take the time to ask questions, check in, and adjust based on how you're feeling that day — sometimes the results are immediate, and sometimes real progress takes a few sessions. Either way, my priority is that you leave feeling better than when you arrived.

Outside of work, my life is full in the best way. I'm a boy mom and a bonus mom in the making, and my kids are at the heart of everything I do. That's a big part of why I love working with fellow moms — I know firsthand the physical toll of carrying, holding, and chasing after little ones, and how easily we put ourselves last. When I'm not on the table, you'll usually find me staying active: going on walks and discovering new parks, or finding new ways to move and challenge myself at the gym. Staying active — on my own and with my kids — keeps me grounded, and it's part of why I care so much about helping you feel good in your body, too.

Above all, I'm grateful for the trust you place in me. I'll always listen, pay close attention, and create a safe, comfortable space for your healing. Your well-being is my highest priority — thank you for letting me be part of it.

Read my approach
Philosophy

The body communicates — my job is to listen

Every appointment is different, because your body needs something different every time you're on the table. Pressure, pace, and technique shift based on what's actually happening — sometimes that calls for Graston or cupping on a stubborn issue, sometimes it calls for slowing everything down.

This work supports people managing headaches, back pain, the toll of a desk job or a job on your feet all day, pregnancy, and the general wear of caring for a household. We hold a lot of tension — old and new — in our muscles, and part of this practice is learning to listen to what yours is telling us.
